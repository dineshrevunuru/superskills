# Position Product — Differentiation From Evidence

Produce ONE narrow, true, demonstrable claim about why this thing (or this person) over the alternatives — plus the proof plan that makes the claim land inside the audience's real attention window. Adjectives are not positioning. Evidence is.

## When to use / when NOT to use

**Use for:** a product's core claim, a feature's "why this matters," a company one-liner, a portfolio thesis, a candidate's "why you" answer. The method is identical for products and people — a person's positioning is a product positioning where the artifact is their shipped work.

**Do NOT use for:**
- Competitor teardown / feature matrix → `run-competitive-analysis` (its output is step 3's input here)
- Narrating one piece of work end-to-end → `tell-case-study-story` (it inherits the thesis from here)
- Auditing whether EXISTING positioning claims hold up → `craft-critique` (this skill creates; that one judges)
- Designing the surface where positioning renders (landing page, onboarding, pricing) → `design-growth-surfaces`
- Final wording and register → `writing-voice`

## Before you start (both mandatory)

1. **Load `craft-critique`.** Every claim about markets, competitors, buyers, recruiters, or trends in this method is handled by its evidence protocol (cite / get / flag) and screened against its banned constructions. Do not restate the protocol — apply it.
2. **Load `design-taste`** if the output will be designed. Its governing phrase — **"quiet surface, loud proof"** — is also the positioning law: restraint stays on the skin; the substance (real metrics, live artifacts, working systems) carries the persuasion.

## Evidence tags (stamp one on every external claim)

| Tag | Meaning | Allowed use in positioning |
|---|---|---|
| `[V-high]` | Survived adversarial verification, multiple independent sources | Load-bearing plank |
| `[V-med]` | Pattern verified; headline numbers vendor/marketing-grade | Supporting plank; never the thesis alone |
| `[V-low]` | Single small study / weak method | Design input only; flag if quoted |
| `[GAP]` | No claim survived checking | Never marketed; becomes a research task |
| `[REFUTED]` | Specifically disproven | Kill list — never appears, even paraphrased |
| `[Assumption]` | Chosen belief, revisable | Stated as an assumption, with what would change it |

## The method

### 1. Fix the audience and the moment
- Name the **decider** — recruiter, buyer, exec, first-time user. Not "users" in general.
- Establish the **attention window with evidence, not hope**. Worked case: a product designer positions their portfolio for a ~55-second recruiter first-pass that rarely includes an off-site click. Rule: **position for the scan; assume the off-site click never happens.**
- Name the **real alternative** they compare you against (incumbent, other candidates, doing nothing) and the **default belief** you must contrast — positioning is a correction to something they already think.
- Different audiences get different moments, not different truths. One fact base; per-audience emphasis.

### 2. Inventory only verified truth
List every fact the story might stand on. Grade each with a tag + source. Then apply three hard filters:
- **Numbers without a named measurement source are presumed fabricated — cut, don't soften.** Real case: plausible-sounding enterprise metrics ("25% satisfaction lift") in a candidate's old portfolio material were fabricated → cut entirely, not rounded down.
- **Quotes and credentials must verify verbatim.** Real case: a widely-repeated expert quote returned zero verbatim matches → the positioning re-anchored on the platform's official design guide instead. If the source can't be checked, swap the source, don't defend it.
- **NDA'd / unshipped work keeps names + scale, drops outcomes.** The "NDA-honest" pattern: client names and scope numbers ("7 clusters, 34 plants") stay; outcome metrics and "shipped to production" go unless verifiable.

Anything that fails a filter becomes either a **GET** (research it before continuing) or is out of the inventory. Positioning built on a fact you'd have to defend is already lost.

### 3. Map the field — supply gap × demand ask
Take `run-competitive-analysis` output, or run a scoped scan: what does every alternative **claim**, and what do they actually **show**?
- The highest-leverage seam is a **convergence**: something deciders explicitly ask for (demand-ask) that nobody in the studied field supplies (supply-gap). Worked case: hiring managers ask for outcomes in line one AND the studied competitor pool was weakest exactly there → "metric in line one" became the single highest-leverage move.
- **Count the field.** "0 of 61–73 studied portfolios host a live on-page AI demo" is a hard-evidenced scarcity claim. "Everyone is weak at this" is an impression — tag it honestly.

### 4. Find the seam — the candidate-claims table
Build this table before writing a word of copy:

| Candidate claim | Evidence (tag + source) | Who else can truthfully say it | Copyable? |
|---|---|---|---|

Copyability has two grades:
- **Structural** (best): the alternative's business model or incentives prevent copying. Worked case: an incumbent shopping assistant that blends sponsored results into conversation cannot become visibly fit-first without giving up a measured revenue lever — so "visibly-reasoned, fit-first" owns space the incumbent *structurally* can't enter.
- **Scarcity-based** (legitimate, decaying): nobody does it *yet*. Valid wedge; handle in step 6.

Pick the claim that is **narrow** (one seam, not general excellence), **true** (survives step 2), **demonstrable** (an artifact or number the audience can touch), and **hardest to copy**.

### 5. Write the thesis — one sentence, contrast form
The reliable shape: **"Most X [the default the audience already believes]. This one [the difference] — and here it is running."**
- Real person example: *"Most product designers hand the AI off to engineers. I design it and I ship it in code — she's live on this page."*
- Real product example: *the shopkeeper who tells you the downside* — an assistant that reasons from your stated needs, pins the runner-up, and refuses what it shouldn't sell.

Rules: no adjective does load-bearing work; the difference must be **checkable within the moment** from step 1; never name or disparage a competitor in the thesis — contrast the *category default*, generically and honestly.

### 6. Separate the scarcity fact from the winning bet (mandatory)
Every positioning output contains this split, explicitly:
- **Hard-evidenced:** the scarcity count, the measured metric, the structural incentive.
- **Opinion/bet:** that this difference will *win*. Almost always trend-supported opinion, not survey data. Say so.
- **Decay:** scarcity wedges lose value as they're copied → the recommendation is usually "ship while the field is empty," stated as a **timing bet**. Structural wedges hold while the incumbent's incentive holds — name the incentive so the expiry condition is visible.

Worked case, verbatim pattern: *"the scarcity is hard-evidenced (the 0-of-61–73 scan); the 'this will win' is trend-supported opinion. Still the right bet — but a bet on timing, and its value decays as others copy it."* That honesty flag IS the deliverable, not a hedge on it.

### 7. Plan the proof surface — quiet surface, loud proof
- **Proof in line one.** The metric, scale anchor, or live artifact leads; process and backstory follow. Never make the audience dig for the reason to believe.
- **Demonstration beats description.** A live, operable artifact collapses "prove the claim" into the scan itself; a link off-site is proof that mostly never gets clicked. If the claim is "we build X," the strongest surface is X, running, on the page.
- **The no-title rule:** minimal proof is a privilege of the already-credentialed — a frontier logo vouches for its people; if the audience doesn't already trust you, **over-prove the substance while keeping the surface quiet** (see `design-taste` for the surface register).
- **Where no metric exists** (NDA, early product): use the scale anchor and make the absence *visible as discipline* — an honest gap reads as integrity; a filled gap reads as fabrication the moment anyone checks.
- **Never market a `[GAP]`/`[REFUTED]` claim, even the emotionally strongest one.** Real case: an elder-companion product never markets loneliness *outcomes* — every study behind them was uncontrolled — so it's marketed as *presence and connection*, never clinical benefit.

### 8. Write the kill list
Enumerate what must never be claimed, with the reason and (where relevant) where the topic IS handled instead:
- `[REFUTED]` claims and fabricated/unsourced numbers
- Unverifiable third-party quotes or credentials
- Capabilities not yet shipped (re-add when shipped, not before)
- Topics deliberately kept off the face (e.g., visa/sponsorship handled in the application form, not the portfolio)
- Viral competitor stats with no method behind them — **foil only, never a cited plank**; find the better-grounded alternative and cite that instead

Positioning is as much refusal as assertion. Write the refusals down so future copy can't drift.

## The differentiator test (all seven, or it's not the thesis)

- [ ] **Narrow** — claims one seam, not general excellence
- [ ] **True** — every load-bearing fact tagged `[V-med]` or better, with source
- [ ] **Demonstrable** — an artifact or number the audience can verify inside the attention window
- [ ] **Scarce, evidenced** — "0 of N studied" beats "few competitors"
- [ ] **Hard to copy** — structural if possible; if scarcity-only, decay stated
- [ ] **Generic-honest contrast** — no competitor named and disparaged
- [ ] **Survives `craft-critique`'s banned-constructions scan** — zero "uniquely positioned" energy

## Worked example — positioning a person (a product designer's portfolio thesis)

1. **Audience & moment:** recruiter/hiring manager · ~55s reviewer first-pass, rarely clicks off-site (the portfolio's working assumption) · compared against AI-role designers at frontier AI companies · default belief: "designers describe AI work; engineers build it."
2. **Truth inventory:** shipped, live AI chatbot with real measured conversion metrics `[V-high — first-party analytics, sources named]` · enterprise work under NDA — client names + scale kept, outcome metrics cut as unverifiable, "shipped to production" claim FALSE → cut · a graduate degree completing on a verified date · plausible-sounding old metrics found fabricated → deleted, not softened.
3. **Field map:** a competitive scan of 61–73 AI-role designer portfolios (incl. frontier-company designers): **0 host a live on-page AI demo** — AI proof is always described or linked off-site `[hard-evidenced scarcity]`. Converges with the demand-ask: reviewers want outcomes in line one.
4. **Seam:** "designs AND ships the AI" — narrow, true (live artifact), demonstrable within the scan (the demo runs on the page), and hard to copy quickly (requires actually building).
5. **Thesis:** *"Most product designers hand the AI off to engineers. I design it and I ship it in code — and you're standing in the proof of it."*
6. **Scarcity vs bet:** scarcity hard-evidenced (the 0-of-N scan); "a live demo wins the role" is trend-supported opinion; value decays as copied → ship while the field is empty, named as a timing bet.
7. **Proof plan:** flagship case leads with the real measured metric in line one · NDA case leads with the scale anchor, no numbers, absence-as-discipline · the live operable demo is the wedge, on-page, so proof lands inside the 55 seconds.
8. **Kill list:** fabricated enterprise metrics (cut) · "uniquely positioned" constructions (banned) · unshipped skills (off until shipped) · sponsorship off the portfolio face (handled in the application form) · nonexistent projects (never referenced).

## Worked example — positioning a product (compressed)

| Step | Honest conversational-commerce assistant (Echo Show, first-time internet users) |
|---|---|
| Audience & moment | Low-literacy first-time buyers who trust **peers, not institutions** `[V-high]` — and the exec approving the product |
| Truth | Honest by **architecture**: zero invented prices by construction; medicine questions refused by construction (verifiable in the build, not a promise) |
| Field | Incumbent assistant blends sponsored results into conversation; ranking opaque to shopper and merchant `[V-high]`; personalization is a measured revenue lever pulling every incumbent sell-first `[V-high]` |
| Seam | **Visibly-reasoned, fit-first recommendation** — structural: the incumbent can't copy it without giving up the revenue lever. "The credibility no blended-ranking assistant can copy." |
| Thesis | The shopkeeper who tells you the downside: reasons from your stated needs, pins the runner-up, refuses what it shouldn't sell |
| Scarcity vs bet | Honesty-by-architecture is verifiable now; "trust converts this segment" rests on peer-influence data `[V-med]` + a bet — flagged |
| Kill list | No loneliness-outcome claims (`[REFUTED]` marketing — every study uncontrolled) · the viral "incumbent is 83% self-serving" stat = single vendor quote, no method → narrative foil only; cite the grounded 1,300-pick analysis instead |

## Anti-patterns

| Don't | Why it fails | Do |
|---|---|---|
| "Uniquely positioned…" / "strong foundation…" | Banned per `craft-critique` — fits anyone | One narrow checkable claim |
| Adjective positioning ("seamless," "best-in-class") | Unfalsifiable = unpersuasive | Lead with the artifact or number |
| Filling an NDA/metric hole with a plausible number | Fabrication detected = everything else discounted | Scale anchor + visible absence as discipline |
| Resting the story on an unverified quote/credential | Collapses when checked | Verify verbatim or swap to the official citable source |
| Naming and trashing the competitor | Reads insecure; legal/aggro risk | Contrast the category default, generically |
| Leading with process/backstory | The scan ends before the proof arrives | Proof in line one |
| Treating "this will win" as evidenced because scarcity is | Conflates fact with bet | Split them; state the decay |
| Marketing the `[REFUTED]`-but-moving claim | One checked claim poisons all claims | Kill list, enforced |
| One positioning blob for every audience | Nobody's moment is served | One fact base, per-audience emphasis (step 1) |
| Differentiation buried in a feature list | Ten claims = no claim | One seam, one sentence |

## Output format

```markdown
## Positioning: [product / person / feature]

**Audience & moment:** [decider · attention window (evidence) · compared against · default belief]

**Thesis:** [one contrast sentence — "Most X… This one… — and here it is running."]

**Claims table:**
| Candidate claim | Evidence (tag + source) | Who else can say it | Copyable? |

**Scarcity vs bet:** hard-evidenced: […] · opinion/bet: […] · decay/expiry condition: […]

**Proof plan:** [line-one proof per surface; live demonstration > description; absence-as-discipline where no metric exists]

**Kill list:** [claims that never appear + why + where the topic is handled instead]

**Under-evidenced flags:** [every `[GAP]`/`[Assumption]` + exactly what data would strengthen each]
```
